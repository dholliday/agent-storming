# Example — Insurance underwriting

A complete, Process-level Agent Storming board for an **AI-assisted insurance underwriting** flow: an application comes in, agents extract it, score the risk, price it, a human underwriter signs off the risky ones, and a policy is issued. Every sticky uses its canonical [grammar colour](../guide/grammar.md); the violet frame is a **swarm**, the teal frame a **fleet**, and the dashed pink line a **milestone**.

## How to read it

Walk it left to right, speaking the [sentence templates](../guide/grammar.md#45-sentence-templates-the-grammar-that-checks-itself) out loud:

1. **When** a *new application is submitted* (trigger), **start** *Extract application data* (task), dispatched to the **Intake Agent**. It reads the *submission packet*, calls an *OCR / document parser* (tool), and is gated by a *PII redaction & schema* **guardrail**. During renewal season it scales out as a **fleet of 10**.
2. The **Risk Assessment Agent** reads *actuarial tables* and *loss & claims history*, calls the *credit bureau* and *fraud-signal* APIs, and produces *Risk score computed* — behind a *confidence ≥ 0.8 or escalate* guardrail. The red **hotspot** flags the open risk: hallucinated factors and fraud false-positives.
3. A **routing policy** fans out by risk band: low → straight-through quote, medium → the **Pricing Agent** (rating engine + rate-cap guardrail), high → a human. The dashed pink **milestone** marks the underwriting decision.
4. A **Senior Underwriter** (human-in-the-loop) reviews and signs off the risky / large-limit cases — the branch produces either *Policy approved* or *Application declined*.
5. On approval, a policy routes to the **Document Agent**, which calls *DocGen + e-signature* and produces *Policy issued*.

All four agents sit inside one **Underwriting Swarm**, orchestrated end-to-end.

### What the board surfaces

- The misclassification / fraud **hotspot** is unresolved — a candidate for a "route to human on low confidence" policy (it partly exists via the confidence guardrail).
- Money and customer-facing decisions have a **human checkpoint** (the underwriter) and **guardrails** (rate caps, PII) — the board would be *incomplete* without them.
- The **Risk Assessment Agent** is a single point of failure in the swarm; the fleet scales *intake* throughput but not *risk* logic. Worth a note before build.
